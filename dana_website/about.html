About DANA

Every neighborhood deserves a voice.

Downtown has changed. So has the neighborhood. For decades, downtown was somewhere people visited. Today, thousands of people call it home.

Why DANA Exists

DANA exists so residents have a clear way to understand, connect, and take part.

We are a volunteer-led neighborhood association for the people who live downtown. Some neighbors first meet us at a happy hour. Others come through a public meeting, a safety concern, a park cleanup, or a question about what is changing nearby.

We are not trying to stop change. We are helping residents shape it.

How Residents Use DANA

Start with the thing you are trying to understand.

DANA is useful when a neighbor has a question, a concern, or a reason to show up. The work is not abstract. It is the practical middle ground between daily life downtown and the public decisions that shape it.

What We Do

Downtown changes all the time. New buildings are proposed. Roads are redesigned. Parks improve. Businesses open. New neighbors move in.

DANA helps residents understand those changes, connect with one another, and have a voice in conversations that shape the future of downtown.

Most importantly, we help turn a busy city center into a neighborhood.

What Matters To Us

Everything we do comes back to everyday life downtown.

  • Housing
  • Public safety
  • Transportation
  • Parks and public spaces
  • Walking and cycling
  • Neighborhood events
  • Community connections

If it affects people living downtown, it matters to us.

How We Work

DANA is run almost entirely by volunteers. Board members, committee members, and residents give their time because they care about the neighborhood.

Some organize events. Some meet with City staff. Some write newsletters. Others simply welcome new neighbors. Every contribution helps.
