Leadership

Meet the neighbors helping DANA run well.

DANA is led by downtown residents who volunteer their time, relationships, and know-how to keep the neighborhood heard.

Board Leadership

Cyrus Tehrani, President

Hanover Brazos

Cyrus works with city staff, partner groups, and fellow board members so downtown residents stay part of the conversation on big planning decisions, including the Central City District Plan.

Kimberly Levinson, Vice President

Four Seasons Residences

Kimberly is a connector. She helps bring residents, downtown organizations, and community partners into the same room, and keeps DANA welcoming for people who are new to the conversation.

Roger L. Cauvin, Treasurer

Plaza Lofts

Roger keeps an eye on the numbers and the long view, helping DANA use its resources carefully while supporting programs that make downtown easier to live in.

Kara Young, Secretary

The Independent

Kara helps make DANA easier to find, follow, and join. Her work focuses on clear updates and a friendly first step for neighbors who want to get involved.

Meg Dude, Director

The Shore

Meg brings a digital strategy lens to the board. She works with fellow directors to make DANA's website, membership path, and everyday tools clearer, simpler, and more useful for residents.

How The Board Works

The board works best when it is practical: listen first, keep records clear, and make it easier for residents to know what is happening.

Current work includes planning updates, public safety, sound concerns, sponsorships, and better ways for neighbors to know where to go next.

Clearer Ways To Join In

DANA is tightening up its communications so new neighbors can understand the work, find the right meeting, and take part without needing an inside track.

A Steady Voice Downtown

The board keeps attention on the issues residents feel day to day: safety, noise, streets, public spaces, and the future shape of downtown.
