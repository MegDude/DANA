Leadership

Collaborative leadership for a modern neighborhood association.

Following the June 2026 board transition, DANA has moved toward a collaborative, project-based leadership model that brings each director's professional strengths into shared board work.

Executive Leadership Team

Cyrus Tehrani, President

Hanover Brazos

Cyrus works closely with city staff, partner organizations, and fellow board members to ensure resident perspectives remain represented in major planning initiatives affecting downtown, including the Central City District Plan. He also supports the board's internal modernization work through improved collaboration tools and shared digital workspaces.

Kimberly Levinson, Vice President

Four Seasons Residences

A former two-term President, Kimberly focuses on building relationships across downtown organizations, residents, and community partners while helping ensure DANA remains an accessible and welcoming voice for everyone who calls downtown home.

Roger L. Cauvin, Treasurer

Plaza Lofts

Roger provides financial stewardship for the organization, helping align budgets and resources with DANA's long-term priorities while supporting responsible investment in community programs. He also collaborates with fellow board members on resident feedback for projects such as the Pike Powers Plaza Master Plan.

Kara Young, Secretary

The Independent

Kara works with the communications and technology teams to improve how residents discover DANA, receive updates, and become involved. Her focus is creating a welcoming experience that helps new members quickly become active participants in the downtown community.

Board of Directors

Meg Dude, Director

The Shore

Meg brings expertise in digital strategy, AI, and customer experience design. Working alongside fellow board members, she is helping guide DANA's digital modernization, including the redesign of the website, member experience, and supporting technology platforms that will improve engagement, streamline operations, and create a stronger foundation for future growth.

Strategic Alignment

The current leadership team has prioritized Trust as Currency, a principle reflected in DANA's commitment to transparent data migration and community-first advocacy for downtown livability.

The board's current modernization work connects governance, resident communication, CRM migration, sponsorship, accessibility, and public safety advocacy.
