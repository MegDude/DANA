Leadership

Meet the neighbors behind DANA.

DANA is run by people who live downtown and care about where they live. Every board member volunteers their time because strong neighborhoods do not happen by accident.

Meet the Board

Cyrus Tehrani

President

Cyrus works closely with board members, City staff, and community organizations to make sure downtown residents are part of important conversations about the future of the neighborhood.

Focus areas: Long-term planning, City relationships, neighborhood advocacy, community partnerships.

Kimberly Levinson

Vice President

Kimberly has a knack for bringing people together. She helps introduce residents, work with local organizations, and make DANA feel welcoming and approachable.

Focus areas: Community building, membership, partnerships, resident engagement.

Roger L. Cauvin

Treasurer

Roger looks after DANA's finances with the care people expect from a neighborhood organization. His focus is making sure resources support work that genuinely improves life downtown.

Focus areas: Financial stewardship, governance, long-term planning, organizational sustainability.

Kara Young

Secretary

Kara helps keep DANA organized, accessible, and easy to follow. From meeting notes to resident updates, she makes it easier for neighbors to stay informed and take part.

Focus areas: Communications, member experience, governance, organizational systems.

Meg Dude

Director

Meg focuses on improving the way residents interact with DANA. Her work centers on digital strategy, communication, and making it easier for people to discover events, join the organization, and stay connected.

Focus areas: Digital strategy, communications, membership experience, community engagement.

How The Board Works

The board meets throughout the year to discuss neighborhood priorities, upcoming events, and issues affecting residents.

While each director brings different experience, every decision starts with the same question: is this good for the people who live downtown?

Current Priorities

Building community, improving communication, supporting safer streets and public spaces, following major planning projects, and growing membership.
