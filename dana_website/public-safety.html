Update

Recently Updated

Public safety remains an active DANA priority across meetings, member notes, and issue tracking.

Downtown should be lively and livable.

Music, restaurants, visitors, offices, and homes all belong downtown. DANA pushes for the balance: businesses can thrive, and residents can still sleep.

  • Clear sound rules: DANA backs sound limits people can measure and enforce, along with tools that help venues stay within them.
  • Fair permit review: When a venue repeatedly creates problems, DANA brings resident concerns into the city process.
  • Better buildings: DANA backs housing rules that make apartments easier to live in, with better windows, light, and layouts.

Streets That Feel Human

Whether you walk, roll, bike, drive, or take transit, downtown streets should feel safe and easy to use.

  • Safer crossings: We push for better lighting, repainted crosswalks, and safer routes at busy intersections like Guadalupe and Cesar Chavez.
  • Better walking space: On streets like East 6th, DANA wants more room for people on foot.
  • Street-level care: Parking and blank walls should not make downtown sidewalks feel dead or unsafe.

What This Work Looks Like

  • I-35: Asking for the air-quality work and safety planning residents deserve.
  • Housing: Supporting more homes and more choices downtown.
  • Noise: Protecting local rules that help residents sleep.
  • City partners: Keeping lines open with APD and sound enforcement when neighbors report problems.

A Better Night's Sleep

The most common complaint we hear is simple: the bass is too loud, too late. Live music matters. So does sleep.

  • The issue: Residents report loud music and vibrating floors well past legal cutoff times.
  • What DANA does: We object to repeat offenders and ask the city to follow through before and after permits are approved.
  • What helps: Sound monitors and volume limiters can keep music enjoyable without making homes impossible to sleep in.

Safer Blocks

  • Walking: Better lighting, clearer crossings, and streets designed for people.
  • Buildings: Shared safety ideas for downtown high-rises.
  • Public space: Long-term housing and social services are part of keeping downtown humane and usable.

Key Insight

A healthy downtown is not just an entertainment district. It is a neighborhood.
