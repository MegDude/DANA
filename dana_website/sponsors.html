Community Partners

The neighbors and local businesses helping DANA do the work.

DANA is powered by residents, volunteers, residential buildings, local businesses, and community organizations that care about downtown Austin.

Why It Matters

Good partners help keep the neighborhood connected.

Partner support helps DANA host events, share resident updates, keep meetings accessible, and make it easier for neighbors to understand what is happening downtown.

Some partners are residential buildings. Some are small businesses. Some are civic and cultural organizations. Together, they help make downtown feel more like a neighborhood.

How Partners Help

Support can look a few different ways.

DANA welcomes support from buildings, businesses, civic groups, and neighbors who want downtown to work better for the people who live here.

Buildings

Residential members

Buildings that help residents stay informed, meet neighbors, and take part in downtown conversations.

Businesses

Local supporters

Businesses that invest in a stronger, more connected downtown community.

Community

Civic partners

Organizations working alongside residents on parks, public spaces, events, and neighborhood life.

Support

Program sponsors

Supporters who help fund outreach, meetings, communications, and volunteer projects.

What Support Makes Possible

Small pieces of support add up.

01

Neighborhood Gatherings

Happy hours, meetings, volunteer projects, and welcome tables that help residents meet people nearby.

02

Useful Resident Updates

Clear notes about safety, parks, streets, transportation, development, and decisions worth following.

03

Committee Work

Practical support that helps turn resident concerns into a meeting, letter, partnership, or next step.

04

Better Participation Tools

Easier ways for people to join, volunteer, subscribe, attend events, and stay close to the work.
